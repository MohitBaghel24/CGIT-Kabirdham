Message From The

Principal's Desk

Mr. Vimal Singh Bind

Incharge Principal, CGIT Kabirdham

Welcome to CGIT Kabirdham! We are entering an exciting new era, expanding our legacy from a premier diploma institute to an IIT-like model offering new B.Tech degrees. Our commitment is to foster innovation and empower the next generation of engineers from the 2025-26 session onwards.

Our motto is “Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached.” We are committed towards producing quality diploma engineers and B.Tech graduates to cater for the needs of industry, society, and the country.

About The Institute

Our History & Facilities

The institute was established on 2007, to meet the emerging need of skilled diploma engineers and for the development of technical education in the distant tribal area of Chhattisgarh.

At present there are 03 branches of engineering with an intake of 30 students in each branch: Diploma in Electrical Engineering, Diploma in Electronics & Telecommunication, and Diploma in Computer Science & Engineering. Students can also take admission in lateral entry. The total intake of students per year is 90, and currently, there are more than 115 students pursuing diplomas in this premier institute.

Campus & Infrastructure

The institute has its own building located in a lush green campus which is spread over 15 acres of land. The campus comprises of more than 40 rooms and fully equipped laboratories with most modern equipment based on latest technology for different disciplines of engineering.

Our Faculty & Affiliation

Currently the institute has 10 highly qualified lecturers and 13 supporting staff dedicatedly committed to impart quality technical, academic and professional education. The diploma courses of this institute are recognized by All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE). Presently, this institute is affiliated to Chhattisgarh Swami Vivekananda Technical University (CSVTU), Bhilai.

The institute enjoys a strong relationship with major industries of Chhattisgarh and is firmly committed to its responsibility towards the society. Many social service projects are undertaken to act as a focal point of development for the rural community and generate self-reliance among under-privileged and unemployed rural youth.
